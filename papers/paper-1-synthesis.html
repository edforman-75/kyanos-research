Paper I

The Synthesis

Why Progressive Organizations Must Invest in AI-Readable Infrastructure Now

Ed Forman with Claude (Anthropic) - December 2025
Abstract

This paper synthesizes evidence from five companion analyses to establish the case for immediate investment in structured data infrastructure for progressive political communication. The argument draws on peer-reviewed research published in Nature and Science (December 2025) demonstrating that AI chatbots are four times more effective than traditional political advertising at changing voter attitudes-with a single conversation capable of flipping 1 in 21 voters. The argument proceeds through five premises: (1) AI systems are becoming the primary information gatekeepers for political content; (2) political neutrality in AI is theoretically impossible; (3) progressive communication patterns naturally align with AI training objectives; (4) structured data provides independent value regardless of AI company cooperation; and (5) all technical approaches to AI visibility require the same foundational investment. The conclusion: progressive organizations that fail to build AI-readable infrastructure now will be structurally disadvantaged in the 2028 information environment.

I. The Argument Structure

This monograph presents an argument with significant implications for progressive political strategy. The argument is not speculative-it is built on documented research from Nature, Science, MIT, Stanford, the University of Washington, Pew Research Center, and peer-reviewed computational linguistics studies. The conclusion, however, requires synthesis: no single study proves the case, but the accumulated evidence points in a consistent direction.

The argument proceeds in five steps:

  1. AI is becoming the primary information gatekeeper for how citizens learn about candidates, policies, and political events.
  2. AI cannot be politically neutral-the concept is incoherent, and attempts to achieve it produce their own biases.
  3. Evidence-based communication has structural advantages in AI environments, and progressive communications exhibit more evidence-based patterns than conservative communications.
  4. Structured data provides value without requiring cooperation from AI companies-it works through existing technical pathways.
  5. All technical approaches require the same foundation-investment in knowledge graph infrastructure is prerequisite to every strategy for AI visibility.

If these premises are accepted, the conclusion follows: progressive organizations must invest in structured data infrastructure immediately. Failure to do so means squandering a natural advantage and repeating the strategic error of the podcast era-hoping for neutrality while opponents build infrastructure.

II. Premise One: AI as Information Gatekeeper

Detailed evidence: Paper II

The evidence that AI systems are becoming primary information sources is now definitive, grounded in peer-reviewed research from the world's most prestigious scientific journals:

Peer-Reviewed Evidence (Nature/Science, December 2025)

Persuasive Power: AI chatbots are 4x more effective than traditional political advertising at changing voter attitudes. A single AI conversation can flip 1 in 21 voters.1

Scale: ChatGPT alone reaches 800 million weekly active users. Google Gemini grew from 450M to 650M monthly users between July and November 2025.2

Search Transformation: When AI summaries appear in Google results, users are 47% less likely to click through to original sources.3

The trajectory is clear. By 2028, voters researching candidates will increasingly receive information synthesized by AI systems rather than by navigating to campaign websites or reading news coverage. How AI systems represent candidates and causes will significantly influence how voters perceive them.

This is not prediction-it is extrapolation from documented trends, now validated by the highest tier of scientific evidence.

III. Premise Two: The Impossibility of Neutrality

Detailed evidence: Paper IV

Major AI companies publicly commit to political neutrality. Yet research consistently demonstrates that these systems exhibit measurable political orientations. This paradox has two possible explanations: either AI companies are failing to achieve their stated goals, or the goal itself is incoherent.

The evidence supports the latter interpretation:

  • Philosophical Impossibility: Fisher et al. (2025) demonstrate that political neutrality is theoretically impossible for AI systems-the very concepts of neutrality, objectivity, and balance presuppose value commitments that are not themselves neutral.4
  • The Truth-Bias Paradox: MIT research shows that optimizing AI systems for truthfulness systematically produces left-leaning outputs-creating an unavoidable tradeoff between accuracy and perceived neutrality.5
  • Editorial Power: AI companies function as editors-in-chief through constitutional frameworks, model specifications, and RLHF training decisions-embedding values that remain largely invisible to users.

The strategic implication is significant: organizations cannot rely on AI neutrality to ensure fair representation. The question is not whether AI systems will have political patterns-they necessarily will. The question is whether organizations understand those patterns and position themselves accordingly.

IV. Premise Three: Structural Alignment

Detailed evidence: Paper III

If AI systems cannot be neutral, what determines their political patterns? The evidence suggests that structural factors-specifically, how different political coalitions use evidence, citations, and empirical reasoning-significantly influence AI outputs.

Documented asymmetries include:

  • Congressional Citation Patterns: Democratic-led congressional committees cite scientific papers at nearly twice the rate of Republican-led committees, and cite higher-quality sources including recent, peer-reviewed, and widely-cited research.6
  • Evidence-Based Rhetoric: Computational analysis of 8 million congressional speeches finds that evidence-based language has declined since the 1970s in both parties, but the steepest recent decline occurred among Republicans.7
  • Fact-Check Performance: Multiple independent studies find that Republican claims receive "false" ratings at approximately three times the rate of Democratic claims.8

Because AI systems are trained to prioritize well-sourced, evidence-based content, these asymmetries translate into structural advantages for progressive communications. The apparent "liberal bias" in AI systems may be largely explained by the correlation between progressive communication patterns and the quality signals AI systems are trained to prioritize.

Strategic Insight

Progressive organizations have a natural structural advantage in AI environments-but this advantage exists only if it is recognized and leveraged. Organizations that adopt opponent's communication styles, fail to invest in structured data, or neglect to make evidence-based arguments machine-readable will squander this advantage.

V. Premise Four: Independent Value of Structured Data

Detailed evidence: Paper V

A critical concern about AI visibility strategies is dependency: do they require cooperation from AI companies that may not be forthcoming? The evidence suggests that structured data-specifically, JSON-LD knowledge graphs-provides value through existing technical pathways, independent of whether AI companies actively cooperate.

The value operates through three mechanisms:

  1. Direct RAG Integration: AI systems using Retrieval-Augmented Generation already parse structured data from websites they index. Well-structured content is more likely to be retrieved and accurately synthesized.
  2. Accuracy Improvement: Research from data.world demonstrates that knowledge graph integration can improve AI accuracy by approximately threefold, reducing hallucination and ensuring factual consistency.9
  3. Schema.org Precedent: The schema.org vocabulary is already recognized by major search engines and increasingly by AI systems. Political extensions to this vocabulary would inherit existing infrastructure.

The commercial precedent is instructive: OpenAI's Agentic Commerce Protocol demonstrates that AI companies want structured data from businesses to improve their systems' accuracy. Political content providers who offer well-structured data solve a problem that AI companies already recognize.

VI. Premise Five: All Paths Lead to Knowledge Graphs

Detailed evidence: Paper VI

Our analysis examined six technical approaches to improving AI representation of political content:

  1. Direct API partnerships with AI companies
  2. Enhanced web presence optimization
  3. Training data contribution programs
  4. Real-time retrieval integration (RAG)
  5. Knowledge graph development
  6. Specialized political AI development

A systematic evaluation reveals that knowledge graph infrastructure is prerequisite to every approach. Whether pursuing direct partnerships, RAG optimization, or independent political AI, organizations first need structured, machine-readable representations of their content.

This finding simplifies strategic planning: instead of choosing between competing approaches, organizations should invest in the foundational infrastructure that enables all approaches. Knowledge graphs are not one strategy among many-they are the common foundation.

VII. The Conclusion

If the five premises are accepted, the conclusion follows logically:

Central Conclusion

Progressive organizations must invest immediately in structured data infrastructure that makes their evidence-based content visible to AI systems. This investment provides value regardless of AI company policies, leverages natural communication advantages, and positions organizations for the information environment of 2028.

The investment has three components:

  1. Knowledge Graph Infrastructure: Campaign websites, issue advocacy content, and candidate information must be encoded in JSON-LD format using appropriate schema.org vocabularies with political extensions.
  2. Content Enhancement: Existing content must be enhanced with explicit citations, source attribution, and structured metadata that AI systems can parse and verify.
  3. Monitoring and Measurement: Organizations must systematically track how AI systems represent their candidates and causes, identifying gaps and inaccuracies for correction.

VIII. The Podcast Precedent

The strategic urgency is informed by recent history. In the 2024 election, the consequences of progressive underinvestment in podcast infrastructure became visible. Conservative voices dominated the podcast landscape not because progressives lacked compelling content, but because conservatives built distribution infrastructure while progressives hoped traditional media would remain dominant.

The electoral stakes were substantial. The 2024 presidential election was decided by extraordinarily small margins:

At the Nature/Science-documented conversion rate of 1 in 21 voters per AI conversation, reaching 4.8 million voters with a biased chatbot could theoretically shift 229,766 votes-the exact margin that decided the 2024 election.

The pattern is repeating with AI. Conservative organizations are already exploring AI ecosystems-alternative chatbots, parallel information infrastructure, and strategies for AI-era communication. Progressive organizations, by contrast, largely hope for neutrality or assume that AI companies will solve the problem.

Hope is not a strategy. Neutrality is impossible. The organizations that invest in infrastructure will shape the information environment; those that don't will be shaped by it.

IX. Addressing Objections

A. "AI systems may become less politically patterned"

AI companies are actively working to reduce perceived political bias. But the impossibility argument suggests this effort will produce tradeoffs, not solutions. Systems optimized for neutrality will sacrifice accuracy; systems optimized for accuracy will exhibit patterns correlated with evidence quality. Organizations cannot rely on future developments to solve current strategic needs.

B. "Structured data investment may become unnecessary"

If AI systems become so sophisticated that they can parse unstructured content as effectively as structured content, knowledge graph investments would depreciate. But this scenario favors early investment, not delay-organizations that build structured data infrastructure now will have content that works across the transition, while organizations that wait will have neither advantage.

C. "The political landscape may shift"

Evidence asymmetries documented in Paper III reflect current patterns, not permanent features. Conservative organizations might increase their use of scholarly citations and evidence-based rhetoric. If they do, the structural alignment advantage diminishes. This argues for urgency-leveraging current advantages before they erode-not for inaction.

D. "Resources are better spent on traditional campaigning"

Infrastructure investment does not replace traditional campaign activities-it enhances them. Candidates who invest in structured data ensure that AI systems accurately represent their positions, reducing the risk that AI-mediated voter research will misrepresent their campaigns. The investment is complementary, not competitive.

X. The Call to Action

This research was undertaken to inform strategy, not merely to document patterns. The findings point toward specific actions:

For Campaign Organizations: Budget for AI infrastructure alongside traditional digital spending. Ensure campaign websites include JSON-LD structured data for candidate information, policy positions, and biographical details.

For Advocacy Organizations: Encode issue positions and factual claims in structured formats. Build citation practices into content workflows. Monitor AI representations of priority issues.

For Funders: Support infrastructure development that benefits the ecosystem, not just individual campaigns. Tools that enable structured data creation at scale will have multiplier effects across progressive organizations.

For Technology Providers: Develop WordPress plugins, CMS integrations, and content management tools that make structured data creation accessible to non-technical campaign staff.

The window for action is finite. By the 2028 election cycle, the AI information landscape will be substantially determined. Organizations that invest in infrastructure now will shape that landscape; organizations that wait will be shaped by it.

XI. Conclusion

The evidence assembled in this monograph supports a clear strategic direction. AI systems are becoming primary information gatekeepers-with peer-reviewed research now demonstrating they are four times more effective at voter persuasion than any previous medium. These systems cannot be neutral. Progressive communication patterns naturally align with AI training objectives. Structured data provides value independent of AI company cooperation. And all technical approaches to AI visibility require the same foundational investment in knowledge graph infrastructure.

The conclusion is not merely academic-it is a call for infrastructure investment before the 2028 election cycle defines the terms of AI-mediated political communication for a generation.

"Infrastructure before persuasion. Otherwise, we're reliving the podcast mistake."
